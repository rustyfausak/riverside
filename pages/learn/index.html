Stretching

Static Stretching

  • Static stretching does not decrease delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) or risk of injury.
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10593217
      "Stretching before exercise does not reduce the risk of local muscle injury"
    • http://www.bmj.com/content/325/7362/468.full
      "Stretching before exercising does not produce useful reductions in injury risk"
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10694106
      "No significant effect of preexercise stretching on all-injuries risk"
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9764443
      "It is concluded that passive stretching did not have any significant influence on increased plasma-CK, muscle pain, muscle strength and the PCr/P(i) ratio, indicating that passive stretching after eccentric exercise cannot prevent secondary pathological alterations"
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12617692
      "...stretching...has demonstrated no effect on the alleviation of muscle soreness or other DOMS symptoms.
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21735398
      "The evidence from randomised studies suggests that muscle stretching, whether conducted before, after, or before and after exercise, does not produce clinically important reductions in delayed-onset muscle soreness in healthy adults."
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15782063
      "Limited evidence showed stretching had no effect in reducing injuries"
    • http://www.usatf.org/stretchStudy/StretchStudyReport.pdf
      "There was no statistically significant difference in injury rates between the stretch and no stretch groups for any specific injury location or diagnosis"
    • http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24856792
      Stretching increases range of motion but does little to alter the muscle itself.
  • Static stretching does not increase performance.
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21659901
      "short-duration acute static stretch (<30s) has no detrimental effect"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17313299
      "vertical jump performance is diminished for 15 minutes if performed after static or PNF stretching"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23681447
      "acute effect on performance might be limited in practice"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10601154
      "The results suggested that the stiffness of tendon structures has a favorable effect on stretch-shortening cycle exercise, possibly due to adequate storage and recoil of elastic energy."
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20683355
      "it seems harmful to include static stretching in the warm-up protocol"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15903372
      "it is recommended that heavy static stretching exercises of a muscle group be avoided prior to any performances requiring maximal muscle strength endurance."

Dynamic Stretching

  • Dynamic Stretching as a warmup improves performance.
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21373870
      "Dynamic stretching has been shown to either have no effect or may augment subsequent performance"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22067244
      "A dynamic stretching intervention appears to be more suitable for use as part of a warm-up"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15903378
      "it may be desirable for children to perform moderate- to high-intensity dynamic exercises prior to the performance of activities that require a high power output."
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20512065
      "A dynamic warm-up may improve vertical jump performance"
    • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16937960
      "the results of this study indicate a relative performance enhancement with the dynamic warm up"
